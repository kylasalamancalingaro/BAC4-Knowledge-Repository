GCP Delivery Gates + SA Engagement Flow

A business-friendly view of when Solution Architecture should engage during delivery, who owns each step, and what evidence is expected before moving through delivery gates.

This model embeds architecture engagement into formal delivery control points so decisions are clear, risks are managed, and teams know what artifacts are needed before progressing.

Integrated Gate-to-SA Mapping

DoR: Scoping → Discovery

Goal

Confirm the initiative is sufficiently framed for structured discovery.

Key outputs

  • Architecture review request
  • Problem statement and context
  • Scope boundaries and constraints
  • Explicit decision needed during discovery

Pass criteria

  • Business problem agreed
  • SA trigger documented
  • Discovery objectives defined
DD: Discovery → Delivery

Goal

Confirm the design path is selected and delivery can build with controlled risk.

Key outputs

  • Option comparison and trade-offs
  • Recommended architecture approach
  • Risk register and mitigations
  • ADR / decision record

Pass criteria

  • Preferred option approved
  • Major risks documented
  • Backlog reflects architecture decisions
DoD: Delivery → Production

Goal

Ensure implementation is aligned to approved architecture and is production ready.

Key outputs

  • Implementation plan
  • Linked delivery tasks
  • Deviation log, if applicable
  • Quality and control evidence

Pass criteria

  • Solution conforms to design
  • Critical checks satisfied
  • Go-live sign-off obtained where required
BAU: Hypercare → Run

Goal

Close SA engagement and transition to stable operations.

Key outputs

  • Closure note
  • Lessons learned
  • Updated standards or playbooks
  • Final support ownership model

Pass criteria

  • Outstanding risks owned
  • Learnings captured
  • Run team accepts ownership

Gate Review Checklist

  1. Gate objective met? Confirm the intent of DoR, DD, DoD, or BAU is satisfied.
  2. SA step outputs complete? Confirm the relevant SA flow evidence is available.
  3. Owner accountability clear? Confirm who signs, who executes, and who owns follow-ups.
  4. Artifacts versioned and accessible? Confirm ADO/Wiki links are available and current.
  5. Risks and deviations assigned? Confirm open risks, exceptions, and next actions have owners and due dates.

In short: this keeps architecture engagement practical, visible, and tied to delivery decisions — not ad hoc or after the fact.
